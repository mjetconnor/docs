Glossary of SpigitEngage to MindjetGraph Terminology

 

SpigitEngage Terms

Terms in MindjetGraph

Description

Application

(OAuth 2): Client

 The OAuth 2 protocol uses the term "client" to talk about applications that are consumers of the API.  When you see "client ID" and "client secret" in the OAuth documentation, think "application ID" and "application secret".

Thread

Discussion

 In MindjetGraph, comment threads are called "discussions".  Entities of type Discussion contain the list of comments that make up that thread.  The "discussions" property on a thing will give the discussions that pertain to that thing.
 
For SpigitEngage, only Ideas have discussions, and they only have one each.

Attachment

Reference

In MindjetGraph, attachments are given the more general name of "reference".  Attachments to ideas in SpigitEngage show up as Reference entities listed in an idea's "references" property.

Idea Template

uiForm

Idea templates in SpigitEngage are represented in MindjetGraph as Form objects given in the "uiForm" property of ideas and governors.  A single entity will represent the uiForm (the idea template) for a given SpigitEngage community or challenge.

Most of the complexity of idea templates comes from specifying how a form should be rendered when querying data from a user to post a new idea or edit an existing one.  This is why the MindjetGraph Form class is a good fit.

Votes, Reviews and Evaluations

Assessment

 In MindjetGraph, votes, reviews and evaluations are all handled under a common abstraction called an Assessment.  They still show up as familiar entity types -- votes as Vote objects, and reviews and evaluations as Review entities.  But you will find these values in the "assessments" property of the assessed object.
 
In SpigitEngage, only ideas and comments have votes, and only ideas have reviews and evaluations.

Ideas and Users

IdeaScores and UserScores

Generally speaking, the metrics associated with a thing - ideas and users for SpigitEngage - are to be found not in the thing itself but in a separate related "score" object.  For ideas, there are IdeaScores.  For users, there are UserScores.

The concept is that these metrics may be context-dependent.  This is easy to see for users, where their "reputation" metric varies by community.  In today's SpigitEngage, the only context is the inquiry to which the idea was posted.  And so today all ideas have exactly one ideaScore, for the idea in that inquiry.

Community

Community, Inquiry, Challenge and SuggestionBox

The topic SpigitEngage Communities covers this in some detail.  The executive summary is this:

"Regular" type SpigitEngage communities are represented in Mindjet Graph by a combination of a Community entity and a SuggestionBox entity.

"Challenge" type SpigitEngage communities are represented in Mindjet Graph only by Challenge entities.

SuggestionBox and Challenge are both subclasses of Inquiry.

Category

Concept

 SpigitEngage categories are represented by concept entities in MindjetGraph.
 
The Inquiry class, the super-class of Challenge and SuggestionBox, it itself a sub-class of ConceptScheme.  As such, if the SpigitEngage community has any categories defined in it, they will appear in the concepts property of the related MindjetGraph inquiry entity.
 
If any idea has been posted to a SpigitEngage category, this can be found in the idea's subject property.  (Note: subject is defined by the Entity class that Idea sub-classes.)
