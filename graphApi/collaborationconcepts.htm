Collaboration Concepts

Collaboration in many forms comes up frequently when dealing with social innovation.  Regardless of which kind, all collaborations have certain elements in common.

First and foremost, all collaborations have a defined set of collaborators, the participants.  This set of players may be highly restrictive or broadly open to all who want to join, but it is required for all collaborations.  All collaborations also have a defined purpose, although that purpose may be inherent in the kind of collaboration, that is the subclass of Collaboration.

Additionally, collaborations may have a defined process and a defined benchmark that determines success.  Some collaborations may be open ended in these regards, others tightly proscribed.

Classes in this diagram: Collaboration, Community, Discussion, Group, Inquiry, Thing

Prominent subclasses of Collaboration in the MindjetGraph model are Community, Discussion and Inquiry.  Discussions are primarily comment threads where the purpose is to have a dialog about a particular thing.  Inquiries are essentially collaborations around answering a particular question.
