The Semantic Web

The Internet itself is a powerful graph database.  The Semantic Web (aka "Web 2.0" or "Web 3.0") and Linked Data are efforts to unlock the information trapped inside otherwise ubiquitous sources.  These projects marry standards for data structures, such as XML and embedded RDF (RDFa), with standards of meaning in the form of published vocabularies.  Without shared vocabularies, it's just Garbage In, Garbage Out on a Big Data scale.

This is an exciting time for internet vocabularies.  There are broadly accepted standards such as the Dublin Core, FOAF (Friend Of A Friend), SKOS (Simple Knowledge Organization System), and SIOC (Semantically Interlinked Online Communities).  And there are emerging vocabularies like NCO (NEPOMUK Contact Ontology) and SUPER (a business process ontology).  RDF and OWL provide the glue that binds it all together (here's a helpful tutorial).

To this, MindjetGraph adds the SIEF vocabulary -- Social Innovation Enterprise Framework.    The terms and classes defined in this document are the elements of that vocabulary..
