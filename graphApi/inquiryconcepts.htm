Inquiry Concepts

Inquiries are collaborations over ideas, in particular over their generation, evaluation and development.  Once again, there are many different ways one might collaborate over ideation, but we can recognize a solid foundation of common elements across all variations.

Because they are Collaborations, they each have a defined set of participants.  The set of participants may be as specific as a short list of  hand-picked users or as broad as the general public at large.

Every inquiry has an IdeaSet that is the set of ideas involved in that inquiry.  There is no inherent restriction about whether ideas may be involved with multiple inquiries, although particular kinds of inquiries may pose limitations of that sort.

Inquiries also provide a Governor that is used when new ideas are posted to the inquiry.  Because it is possible that ideas may be added to an inquiry that were initially created for another inquiry, it is possible that an idea's governor is not the same one as the inquiry's governor.

Inquiries may also be the foundation of a categorization scheme that plays a part in that inquiry.  To that end, all inquiries are also ConceptSchemes.

At this time, these kinds of Inquiry are defined: SuggestionBoxes and Challenge.

SuggestionBoxes generally pose some kind of question, or possibly a set of related questions as represented by its ConceptScheme.  They are open-ended in time, which is where the name comes from.  It is as if you could walk up to the "suggestion box" at any time and drop an idea in.

Challenges are the most structured of inquiries.  They also generally pose some kind of question, but the activity around responding to the question is structured by time-limited phases that decree what kind of activity is appropriate during that period.
