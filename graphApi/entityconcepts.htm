Entity Concepts

Resources in MindjetGraph are called entities.  Entities are the core elements of MindjetGraph.  All persisted data in MindjetGraph are either entities or properties of those entities.

Because entities are RDF resources, they all have URI identifiers that are global and durable.  This URI may be used at any time, within the limits of access controls of course, to retrieve and interact with an entity.  

This UML diagram illustrates the basic structure.  Resources have "eid" (shorthand for "entity ID") properties that are URIs.  Entity is a kind of Resource (a "subclass", indicated by the open arrowhead in the diagram), which means that entities also have eids.  Entities additionally have "type" properties; their values are Classes.

You can think of the arrows as either RDF statements or property relationships, whichever you prefer.

Data Management and Organization

With graph databases, it is a good idea to avoid thinking in terms of "containership", as in "A contains B" or "B belongs to A".  Better to think of the many ever-changing and often non-exclusive ways that A and B relate to each other.  As we have seen, these relationships are captured by RDF statements, which we also call properties.

Nevertheless, the MindjetGraph model does impose a basic containership model on all of its elements.  In short:

  1. Every entity belongs to a single dataSpace, and has a unique key within that dataSpace.

  2. Every dataSpace belongs to a single gateway, and has a unique key within that gateway.

  3. Every gateway is both identified and reachable by a well-known public URL.

DataSpaces and gateways also have a relationship to tenants.  That is covered in more detail in Community and Tenant Concepts.

Entity IDs

The URIs that identify every entity correspond to the structure just described.  All MindjetGraph entity IDs look like this:

gatewayURL / dataspaceKey / entityKey

Example:

http://xyz.mindjet.com/graph/nyc/u512-312.b

has these components:

gateway URL       http://xyz.mindjet.com/graph
dataspace key     nyc
entity key            u512-312.b

The dataSpace and entity keys may be any string of unreserved URL characters (the ASCII alphanumeric characters, dash, underscore, dot and tilde).  These are purely identifiers.  One should never infer anything from these keys, especially not the entity's type.  The way to get an entity's type is to look at its "rdf:type" property.

Common Entity Properties

All MindjetGraph entities regardless of type maintain these properties: eid, dataSpace, type, creator, created, and status.
