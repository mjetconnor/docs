Concept Class

The Concept class comes directly from the Simple Knowledge Organization System (SKOS) vocabulary, an accepted W3C standard.  Concepts represent structured categorization schemes.  Another way to think of concepts is that they are like entries in a thesaurus.  Concepts is the other side of the spectrum from the use of tags, which are unstructured free-form text annotations.

Equivalent to: skos:Concept

Concept

[ * ]

Concepts that are narrower than this one.  These are also known as hyponyms.  Narrower concepts SHOULD be in the same scheme, but that is not rigidly required.

broader

broaders

Concept

[ * ]

Concepts that are broader than this one.  These are also known as hypernyms.  Broader concepts SHOULD be in the same scheme, but that is not rigidly required.

related

relateds

Concept

[ * ]

Concepts that are related in some way to this one.  This is a fairly lose concept in that the relationship could be of just about any kind.  If a more specific relationship applies, such as narrower, broader or equivalent, that should be used instead of this.
