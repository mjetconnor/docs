Assessment Concepts

Feedback and measurement is an important element of any robust innovation management system.  A great deal of creativity goes into designing new and better ways of capturing and representing this kind of information, but there is a solid common structure to all of these variations.  Things as seemingly diverse as simple up/down votes and comprehensive review forms share the same fundamental structure.

The root concept is an Assessment, which represents some kind of registered opinion about a thing.  Every assessment has these things in common: who made the assessment and when (creator and created), what was assessed (object), a representation of the rendered opinion (valuation), and something that provides a context for what the valuation means (benchmark).

There are two major kinds of assessments (for now) - votes and reviews.  Votes are comparatively simple acts generally represented by a number.  The benchmarks for votes are called Ballots.  The different kinds of ballots, such as UpDownBallot or StarBallot, indicate what kind of vote is expected.

Reviews are assessments that are made by filling out some kind of form.  The form is indicated by a Questionnaire benchmark.  Some forms have an inherent mechanism for generating a numeric measure when the form is filled out.  These are called ReviewForms.  It is important to note that not all assessments result in a numeric measure, as is the case with Questionnaire that are not ReviewForms.
