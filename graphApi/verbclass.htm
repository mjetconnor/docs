Verb Class

Concepts: Activity Concepts

Verbs have a hierarchical relationship with other verbs.  A verb that describes are more precise variant of a more general verb is called a "hyponym" of the broader verb.  Conversely, the more general verb is a "hypernym" to the more specific one.  The Verb class inherits the Concept class, so that the hypernym/hyponym relationships are represented by the broader and narrower properties defined by Concept.

All verbs are also entities and as such have well-known entity keys.  The main vocabulary of MindjetGraph verbs is defined in the "http://purl.org/sief/2013/10/activity/" dataSpace.  The key strings are therefore sufficient to clearly identify the verb.
