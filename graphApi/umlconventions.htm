UML Conventions

UML diagrams are used in these help pages to illustrate the relationships between different types of objects.

A rectangle with a name in it indicates an object of that type.  By convention, class names are always capitalized.

Property relationships are denoted by a simple arrow.  The name of the property is given near or over the arrow.

Sub-class relationships are indicated by a fat arrow .

Collections are indicated by noting the multiplicity near the endpoints of a relationship line.  Collections of member objects are indicated with the UML multiplicity value of "0..*" being shown near the member.
