Community and Tenant Concepts

DataSpaces, Tenants and Communities represent the broad organization of things in a MindjetGraph.  We have already touched on DataSpace in Entity Concepts.  They are the physical containers for entities.  Every entity is "owned" by a dataSpace, so much so that it makes up a part of that entity's ID.  In fact, this is the only place where "ownership" really applies anywhere within MindjetGraph.

Tenants correspond to business entities that are clients of the Mindjet system.  Essentially, tenants correspond to the elements in the Real World that sign up or contract for SaaS services from Mindjet.

Communities are the central organizing principle for user experiences.  The community is essentially the home base of a user experience, providing the launch pad for finding root objects such as inquires or fellow participants, as well as being the base from which things like configuration and branding information are derived.  

Classes in this diagram: Collaboration, Community, DataSpace, Group, Inquiry, Idea, Tenant

Every dataSpace is associated with exactly one tenant.  All entities, which includes all communities, belong to some dataSpace as we discussed above.  By extension, all entities can be associated with exactly one tenant, specifically the tenant for that dataSpace.

The community property for Tenants is just the inverse of this relationship for communities.  For every community that belongs to a given tenant, that tenant has a community property that refers back to the community.

Note that Tenant's community property has a multiplicity of "1..*", meaning that there must be at least one.  Any one tenant may incorporate multiple dataSpaces and multiple communities, but it must have at least one of each.  At its simplest, a MindjetGraph tenant is made up of one each of a tenant, dataSpace and community entity, forming a kind of triangle.  Communities and dataSpaces maybe added as it makes sense.
