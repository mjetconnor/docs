Idea Concepts

Ideas are the center of the innovation universe.  A simple definition for what an "idea" is:  "A unit of innovation."  Or maybe: "A unit of proposed innovation."  Riedl et al dug into this in some detail and came up with this:

An explicit description of an invention or problem solution with the intention of implementation as a new or improved product, service, or process within an organization.

An aspect of ideas is that they tend to accumulate information over time.  The evolution of ideas over time, in response to feedback and assessments of various sorts, is often a key part of a comprehensive innovation management process.

A picture of an idea, in the abstract, looks like an identity in the classic RDF sense, at the center of a flurry of activity.  Ideas often start life as simple nuggets but accumulate more and more relationships as they mature.

An interesting thing to note is how much of this picture is defined generically for any MindjetGraph entity.  The constructs for assignments, assessments, teams and discussions can be applied to many things, such as comments, activities or even users.  But ideas are first among equals as a consumer of many of these features.

Ideas can be grouped together into IdeaSets.  A given idea may be a member of multiple ideaSets.  Membership to some ideaSets may be only by explicit action.  Membership to others may be by inference or process, such as a search rule.  Inquiries track their participating ideas through the use of ideaSets.

Classes in this diagram: Idea, IdeaSet, Inquiry

An important aspect of ideas in an innovation system is that they evolve over time in response to feedback in an effort to become the best ideas possible.  Governors define the ecosystem in which this happens, the rules by which an idea may change.  Some of these changes are automatic, such as graduation of ideas to a higher stage when certain criteria are met.

Classes in this diagram: Form, Governor, Idea, Inquiry, LifeCycle, Stage

Although a given idea can be a member of multiple ideaSets and inquiries, it can only evolve one way.  Therefore every idea is associated with exactly one governor that defines and drives that idea's evolution.  Inquiries also have an associated governor.  This is the one that will be used when creating a new idea in the context of that inquiry.
